Solar Energy

Electricity generated via photovoltaic cells. India ranks among the world's top solar markets.

Share of renewables44%
How it works

Photovoltaic panels absorb sunlight and convert photons into direct current (DC), then inverted to AC for grid supply.

Key Regions

Rajasthan, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu — home to large-scale solar parks including Bhadla (2.7 GW).

Wind Energy

Turbine-based power harnessing kinetic wind energy. India is the 4th largest wind power nation globally.

Share of renewables26%
How it works

Wind turbines use aerodynamic blades to convert wind kinetic energy into mechanical rotation, then into electricity via generators.

Key Regions

Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Karnataka. Tamil Nadu alone contributes ~10 GW, the most in India.

Hydro Energy

Dam-based power from flowing water. One of India's oldest and most reliable clean energy sources.

Share of renewables27%
How it works

Potential energy stored in reservoirs is released through penstocks to spin turbines and generate electricity.

Key Regions

Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Arunachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir — where Himalayan rivers provide immense flow.

Nuclear Energy

Fission-based baseload power. India targets 22 GW by 2031 under its three-stage nuclear programme.

Share of renewables4%
How it works

Uranium fuel undergoes controlled fission, generating heat to produce steam that drives turbine-generators.

Key Plants

Kudankulam (Tamil Nadu), Tarapur (Maharashtra), Kaiga (Karnataka), Rawatbhata (Rajasthan), Narora (UP).
